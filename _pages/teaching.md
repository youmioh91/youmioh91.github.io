## Research Mentoring

Research mentoring is a central part of my educational work. I have mentored
postdoctoral researchers, graduate students, and undergraduate researchers working
across atmospheric science, ecology, Earth system modeling, and artificial
intelligence.

My mentoring approach emphasizes:

- clearly defined scientific questions;
- regular individual and group meetings;
- increasing independence as projects progress;
- reproducible coding and data-management practices;
- scientific writing and presentation skills;
- collaboration across disciplinary backgrounds; and
- a supportive research environment grounded in belonging and open communication.

## Guest Lectures and Teaching

I regularly contribute to undergraduate and graduate education through invited
guest lectures, research seminars, and interdisciplinary workshops that connect
fundamental Earth system science with current research.

Selected guest lectures include:

- **University of Colorado Boulder (2026)**  
  *GEOG 5100 – Special Topics: Carbon Cycle*  
  Guest lecture on the global methane cycle, atmospheric inverse modeling, and CarbonTracker-CH₄.

- **Yale University, School of the Environment (2023)**  
  *ENV 623 – Climate Change: Science and Solutions*  
  Guest lecture on methane and climate disruption, highlighting recent advances in atmospheric observations and global methane source attribution.

- **Purdue University (2019)**  
  *EAPS 591 – Stable Isotopes*  
  Guest lecture on stable isotope applications for understanding the global methane cycle.

- **Purdue University (2019)**  
  *EAPS 529 – Modeling Ecosystems and Biogeochemical Cycles*  
  Guest lecture on microbial methane oxidation and its role in regulating Arctic methane emissions under climate change.

Beyond formal classroom teaching, I regularly present tutorials and educational
sessions through interdisciplinary workshops, including AI4Methane activities,
where I introduce students and early-career researchers to atmospheric methane
observations, Earth system modeling, CarbonTracker-CH₄, and artificial
intelligence applications in environmental science.

These lectures emphasize connecting foundational concepts with current research,
allowing students to engage directly with observations, computational tools, and
open scientific questions that shape modern Earth system science.

## Educational and Community Leadership

My educational contributions extend beyond individual mentoring and classroom
teaching. I help create scientific communities in which students and early-career
researchers can learn, collaborate, and develop professional networks.

### AI4Methane

As Principal Investigator of the
[AI4Methane Working Group](https://ai4ch4.com/){:target="_blank"},
I coordinate an interdisciplinary community connecting environmental scientists,
modelers, and artificial-intelligence researchers. The group supports collaborative
research, student participation, workshops, shared datasets, and open-source tools
for methane science.

### NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory Seminar Series

As an organizer of the NOAA GML Seminar Series, I help bring researchers and
students together to discuss emerging topics in atmospheric composition, climate,
observations, and modeling.

### CIRES Mentoring Program

Through the CIRES Mentoring Program, I contribute to efforts that support
professional development, career guidance, and an inclusive research environment.

### Workshops and Scientific Sessions

I have organized interdisciplinary workshops and scientific sessions for the
American Geophysical Union and other research communities. These activities create
opportunities for students and early-career scientists to present their work,
receive feedback, and build collaborations.

### Courses I Would Like to Develop

**Data-Driven Atmospheric Modeling**  
Students would combine observations, physical understanding, statistical methods,
and computational models to investigate atmospheric composition and climate.

**Artificial Intelligence for Weather, Climate, and Earth System Science**  
Students would learn how machine learning can complement physical models while
examining generalization, interpretability, uncertainty, and physical consistency.

**Process-Based Earth System Modeling**  
Students would construct simplified models of environmental processes, evaluate
model assumptions, and compare predictions with observations.

**Model–Data Integration for Environmental Science**  
Students would learn approaches for connecting field measurements, remote sensing,
process models, inverse models, and machine learning.
